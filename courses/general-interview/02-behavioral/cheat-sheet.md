## STAR Template

```

  "At [Company], [brief context â€” team, product, timeframe].
   [The challenge/problem/opportunity in 1-2 sentences]."

TASK (15-30 seconds):
  "My responsibility was [specific role/ownership].
   [Why this mattered / what was at stake]."

ACTION (60-90 seconds):
  "First, I [action 1 with specific detail].
   Then, I [action 2 with specific detail].
   I also [action 3 with specific detail].
   [Key decision point or trade-off]."

RESULT (30-60 seconds):
  "As a result, [quantified outcome].
   [Business impact / team impact].
   [Lesson learned or what you would do differently]."
```

**Timing check:** Total should be 2-4 minutes. If over 4, cut the Situation.

## Common Themes Mapped to Questions

### Conflict Resolution
- "Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager."
- "Describe a time you had to push back on a technical decision."
- "How do you handle it when a colleague disagrees with your approach?"
- "Tell me about a time you had to give difficult feedback."

### Leading Without Authority
- "Tell me about a time you led a project where you weren't the manager."
- "How do you influence teams that don't report to you?"
- "Describe a cross-functional initiative you drove."

### Handling Ambiguity
- "Tell me about a time you made a decision with incomplete information."
- "How do you handle unclear or changing requirements?"
- "Describe how you prioritize when everything is urgent."

### Failure and Learning
- "Tell me about a project that failed."
- "What is the biggest mistake you've made professionally?"
- "What would you do differently in hindsight?"

### Mentorship and Growth
- "How do you approach mentoring junior engineers?"
- "Tell me about a time you helped someone grow significantly."
- "What is your code review philosophy?"

### Shipping Under Pressure
- "Tell me about delivering under a tight deadline."
- "How do you decide what to cut when scope is too large?"
- "Describe a trade-off between quality and speed."

### Technical Leadership
- "Tell me about a major architecture decision you drove."
- "How do you build consensus on technical direction?"
- "Describe your design document process."

### Diversity and Inclusion
- "How do you build inclusive teams?"
- "Tell me about a time you addressed bias."
- "How do you ensure all voices are heard?"

| Principle | One-line summary | Story angle |
|-----------|-----------------|-------------|
| Customer Obsession | Start with the customer, work backwards | Customer impact, user research |
| Ownership | Think long-term, act beyond your scope | Fixing things "not your job" |
| Invent and Simplify | Innovate, simplify, look for external ideas | Novel solutions, removing complexity |
| Are Right, A Lot | Strong judgment, seek diverse perspectives | Good decisions backed by data |
| Learn and Be Curious | Never stop learning | Self-directed growth |
| Hire and Develop the Best | Raise the bar, mentor | Hiring decisions, mentorship |
| Insist on Highest Standards | Relentlessly high standards | Quality advocacy |
| Think Big | Bold vision, inspire results | Ambitious projects, big bets |
| Bias for Action | Move fast, calculated risk-taking | Deciding under uncertainty |

| Deliver Results | Focus on inputs, deliver quality on time | Shipping under pressure |

## Red Flag Answers to Avoid

| Red flag | Why it hurts | Better alternative |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| "I can't think of a failure" | Low self-awareness | Pick a real failure with a real lesson |
| "My coworker was the problem" | Blame-shifting | Focus on what YOU did to resolve it |
| "We just shipped it" | No detail on your contribution | Specify YOUR actions and decisions |
| "It went well" (no metrics) | Unsubstantiated claim | Quantify: revenue, time, users, reliability |
| "I just did what I was told" | No initiative or leadership | Show where you added judgment or went beyond |
| "I worked 80-hour weeks" | Glamorizing unsustainable practices | Focus on smart prioritization, not hours |
| Badmouthing a previous employer | Signals disloyalty | Frame as what you learned and what you want next |
| Revealing confidential details | Breach of trust | Anonymize or generalize sensitive specifics |
| "Anyone could have done it" | False humility, unassessable | Own your contribution while crediting the team |

## Story Selection Decision Tree

```
What is the question asking about?
  |
  |-- Conflict / Disagreement?
  |     --> Use a story where you RESOLVED a conflict constructively
  |     --> Show empathy + outcome focus
  |
  |-- Leadership / Influence?
  |     --> Use a story where you drove an outcome WITHOUT authority
  |     --> Show coalition-building + delegation
  |
  |-- Ambiguity / Decision-making?
  |     --> Use a story where you STRUCTURED the unknown
  |     --> Show frameworks + bias for action
  |
  |-- Failure / Mistake?
  |     --> Use a story where you OWNED the failure
  |     --> Show learning + changed behavior
  |
  |-- Mentorship / Growth?
  |     --> Use a story where someone ELSE grew because of you
  |     --> Show investment + their outcomes
  |
  |-- Pressure / Delivery?
  |     --> Use a story where you SHIPPED under constraints
  |     --> Show trade-off decisions + pragmatism
  |
  |-- Technical Decision?
  |     --> Use a story where you DROVE the decision process
  |     --> Show trade-off analysis + consensus-building
  |
  |-- Unknown / Curveball?
        --> Pause 5-10 seconds
        --> Pick the closest theme from above
        --> Ground your answer in a specific example
        --> If truly novel, reason from principles and be genuine
```

**Golden rule:** When in doubt, pick the story with the strongest METRICS and the clearest demonstration of YOUR individual contribution.
