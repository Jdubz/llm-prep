45:00
45 min round

Experience & Goals

You're evaluating: career fit, communication, and technical depth. You want to understand what this person actually DID (not the team), what was hard, and what they learned.

Opening (5 min)

1. "Tell me about yourself and what you're looking for in your next role."

Listen for: Is it under 2 minutes? Does it connect their past to why they want THIS role?

Project Deep Dive (25 min)

Pick ONE of these, then drill into it for the full 25 minutes:

2. "Tell me about the most technically challenging project you've worked on."
"What was YOUR specific role vs the team's?"
"Walk me through the technical architecture — what components were involved?"
"What was the hardest technical decision? What alternatives did you consider?"
"What constraints were you working under?"
"What did you sacrifice and why?"
"What broke or went wrong? How did you handle it?"
"What was the measurable outcome? Can you put a number on it?"
"What would you do differently if you did it again?"
"How did your work make other people more effective?"
3. "Tell me about a technical contribution that allowed others to be successful."
"Who specifically benefited? How do you know?"
"What would have happened if you hadn't done this?"
"Did anyone push back on your approach?"

Career & Motivation (10 min)

4. "Why Stripe? Why this role specifically — not a regular software engineering role?"
Listen for: personal connection to developer experience, not just "great company"
5. "Tell me about a time you disagreed with a team decision. What happened?"
"How did you handle the disagreement?"
"Were you right? How did it turn out?"
"What would you do differently now?"

Candidate Questions (5 min)

6. "What questions do you have for me?"

If they have no questions, or only generic ones ("what's the culture like?"), that's a weak signal. Good candidates ask specific questions about the team, work, or challenges.

35:00
35 min round

Users First & Curious

You're evaluating two qualities: (1) Do they think about the user first? and (2) Are they genuinely curious?

IMPORTANT: Stories must have ZERO overlap with Round 1. If they reuse a story, say: "You mentioned that earlier — can you give me a different example?"

Prompts — Ask 4 or 5 of these (~7 min each)

1. "Tell me about a time you designed a solution starting from the user's perspective rather than the technical architecture."
"What surprised you about the user's perspective?"
"How did you validate that your solution actually solved their problem?"
2. "Tell me about a time you proactively identified a UX or developer experience issue that nobody asked you to fix."
"How did you notice the problem? What made you dig in?"
"What would have happened if you hadn't done anything?"
3. "Tell me about a time you went deep on something out of pure curiosity — not because anyone asked you to."
"What sparked the curiosity?"
"Where did it lead you that you didn't expect?"
"Did anything you learned change how the team works?"
4. "Tell me about a time you changed your approach based on new information. What made you willing to change course?"
"What assumption did you have going in that turned out to be wrong?"
"If a teammate proposed the original approach, how would you convince them to change?"
5. "Tell me about a time you had to balance what the user wanted against what was technically feasible."
"How did you figure out what they actually needed vs what they asked for?"
"Were they happy with the outcome?"
6. "Describe a time you learned something unexpected from a user or stakeholder interaction."
"What did you learn that you still carry today?"
"How did it change your approach going forward?"

Red flags to watch for:
  • Every story starts with "I was asked to..." (not proactive)
  • Can't name what the USER needed — only what the SYSTEM needed
  • "Curiosity" story is actually just doing their job
  • "Changed approach" story doesn't name the wrong assumption
  • Stories overlap with Round 1
  • Can't answer "what surprised you about the user"

Debrief — Discuss Together

1. Which story was the strongest? Why?

2. Which story fell apart under follow-up questions?

3. Were there any stories that overlapped between the two rounds?

4. Did any answer feel rehearsed vs authentic?

5. Was there a moment where they got defensive instead of reflective?

6. Could they name specific things THEY did (not the team)?

7. Overall: would you want to work with this person?
