## Section 4: Questions for Your Interviewers (10 min)

Good questions show genuine interest and help you evaluate whether Stripe is right for you. Below are 15 questions organized by category. Read each one and mark your top 3-4 with a check. Write 1-2 of your own at the end.

### Engineering & Day-to-Day

1. [ ] "What does a typical week look like for a TSE — how is time split between support, projects, and internal collaboration?"
   - *Why it is good:* Shows you are thinking practically about the role, not just the brand. Best for: Experience & Goals round or any interviewer on the TSE team.

2. [ ] "When a TSE identifies a bug or API design issue through support work, what does the path to getting it fixed look like?"
   - *Why it is good:* Tests whether TSEs have real influence on the product. Best for: Any technical interviewer.

3. [ ] "How do you handle on-call and incident response on the TSE team?"
   - *Why it is good:* Shows you understand operational reality. Best for: Any interviewer.

4. [ ] "What tools or internal systems do TSEs use daily that you think are unusually good or unusually painful?"
   - *Why it is good:* Gets an honest answer and shows you care about tooling. Best for: Any technical interviewer.

### Culture & Collaboration

5. [ ] "How are technical disagreements resolved on the team?"
   - *Why it is good:* Maps directly to Collaborate Egolessly. Best for: Any interviewer.

6. [ ] "Can you tell me about a TSE who has grown significantly in the role? What did their trajectory look like?"
   - *Why it is good:* Shows you are thinking long-term and care about growth. Best for: Experience & Goals round.

7. [ ] "How does Stripe keep the 'Users First' principle alive in practice — not just in hiring, but in daily decisions?"
   - *Why it is good:* Shows you have internalized the principles and want to see them in action. Best for: User First & Curious round.

8. [ ] "What is the most interesting or challenging support case you have seen recently?"
   - *Why it is good:* Gets your interviewer talking about real work — builds rapport and gives you signal. Best for: Any TSE interviewer.

### Growth & Career

9. [ ] "What does the path from TSE to senior TSE look like? What differentiates the levels?"
   - *Why it is good:* Shows ambition without arrogance. Best for: Experience & Goals round.

10. [ ] "Is it common for TSEs to move into other roles at Stripe (product engineering, developer advocacy, solutions architecture)?"
    - *Why it is good:* Shows you are interested in Stripe broadly, not just one team. Best for: Experience & Goals round.

11. [ ] "How does Stripe invest in TSE development — conferences, learning time, internal mobility?"
    - *Why it is good:* Shows you value growth. Best for: Experience & Goals round.

### Hard Questions (Stripe says: "Don't be afraid to ask")

12. [ ] "What is the biggest challenge facing the TSE team right now?"
    - *Why it is good:* Shows confidence. Gets a real answer. Best for: Experience & Goals round.

13. [ ] "If you could change one thing about how Stripe works, what would it be?"
    - *Why it is good:* Signals you can handle nuance and imperfection. Best for: Any round with a tenured interviewer.

14. [ ] "How does Stripe balance shipping fast with maintaining the reliability that financial infrastructure demands?"
    - *Why it is good:* Shows you understand the unique tension of the payments domain. Best for: Any technical interviewer.

15. [ ] "What is the hardest part of the TSE role that is not obvious from the outside?"
    - *Why it is good:* Gets honest signal about role fit. Best for: Any TSE interviewer.

## Section 5: Pre-Interview Checklist (5 min)

### The Night Before

- [ ] All STAR stories rehearsed out loud (timed at under 2 minutes each)
- [ ] Top 4 interviewer questions selected and memorized
- [ ] IDE open with clean workspace; can compile and run code in interview language
- [ ] Can clone a private GitHub repo (SSH keys working, or prepared to "Download ZIP")
- [ ] HTTP client library installed and tested (native fetch for Node, requests for Python)
- [ ] Debugger working — can set breakpoints, step through, inspect variables
- [ ] Zoom installed, camera working, mic working, screen sharing tested
- [ ] Second monitor or split-screen arrangement ready
- [ ] Browser bookmarks ready: language docs, Stripe API docs, Stack Overflow
- [ ] Phone on silent; Slack/email/notifications closed
- [ ] Outfit chosen (yes, even for video — it reduces morning decisions)
- [ ] Alarm set with buffer time

### The Morning Of

- [ ] Eat a real meal (not just coffee)
- [ ] Water bottle filled and at desk
- [ ] Snacks ready for breaks between interviews
- [ ] Quick vocal warm-up: say your "Why Stripe?" answer and one STAR story out loud
- [ ] Open IDE, open browser tabs, open Zoom
- [ ] Close everything else (email, Slack, social media, other projects)
- [ ] Take 3 deep breaths
- [ ] Remember: the interviewer is a collaborator, not an examiner

### Between Interviews (you stay on the same Zoom link)

- [ ] Stand up and stretch
- [ ] Drink water
- [ ] Do NOT review notes — trust your preparation
- [ ] Reset your mental state: each interview is independent
