# STAR Method

| Step | What to do |
|------|-----------|
| **Situation** | Set the scene — company, team, project, timeline |
| **Task** | Your specific role and responsibility |
| **Action** | What *you* did (technical decisions, leadership, influence) |
| **Result** | Quantifiable outcome — metrics, impact, what you learned |

Keep answers to 2–3 minutes. Lead with the punchline, then fill in detail.

# Behavioral Question Patterns

**Conflict / Disagreement**
- Show empathy, active listening, data-driven resolution
- "I sought to understand their perspective, then proposed a compromise backed by data"

**Leadership / Influence**
- Driving decisions without authority, mentoring, setting technical direction
- Show impact through others, not just solo work

**Failure / Mistake**
- Own it fully — no deflecting. Focus on what you learned and changed
- "Here's what I'd do differently now"

**Ambiguity / Autonomy**
- Breaking down vague problems, making decisions with incomplete info
- Show structured thinking: identify unknowns, define scope, iterate

**Delivering Under Pressure**
- Prioritization, tradeoffs, communication with stakeholders
- Show calm decision-making, not heroics

# Technical Communication

- **Think aloud**: narrate your reasoning so the interviewer follows along
- **Clarify requirements**: ask about edge cases, constraints, scale before coding
- **State tradeoffs**: "Option A is simpler but Option B scales better because..."
- **Check in**: "Does this direction make sense?" — keep it collaborative
- **Admit gaps**: "I'm not sure about X, but here's how I'd approach it..."
- **Time management**: don't spend 80\% of time on 20\% of the problem

# System Design Flow

1. **Requirements** (3–5 min) — functional + non-functional, clarify scope
2. **Estimation** (2–3 min) — QPS, storage, bandwidth ballparks
3. **High-level design** (10–15 min) — draw components, APIs, data flow
4. **Deep dive** (10–15 min) — interviewer picks areas to explore

**Tips**: start broad, drive the conversation, justify choices, discuss alternatives

# Coding Interview Tips

- **Brute force first**: get a working solution, then optimize
- **Name variables well**: readability matters, avoid single letters (except `i`, `n`)
- **Test your code**: walk through an example manually before submitting
- **Complexity analysis**: state time and space complexity unprompted
- **Edge cases**: empty input, single element, duplicates, negative numbers

# Questions to Ask the Interviewer

**Team & Role**
- What does a typical sprint/week look like for this team?
- What's the biggest technical challenge the team is facing?
- How are architectural decisions made?

**Process & Culture**
- How do you balance shipping speed vs technical quality?
- What does the on-call rotation look like?
- How is feedback given — formal reviews, 1:1s, async?

**Growth**
- What does the path from senior to staff look like here?
- Are there opportunities to lead cross-team initiatives?

# Negotiation

- **Anchor high**: first number sets the range — research market rates first
- **Competing offers**: the strongest lever — "I have an offer at X for Y"
- **Total compensation**: base + equity + sign-on + benefits — optimize the full package
- **Don't accept immediately**: "I'm excited — I'd like a few days to review the full offer"
- **Negotiate specifics**: start date, remote flexibility, equity refresh schedule, level
- **Be collaborative**: "I want to make this work" — not adversarial
